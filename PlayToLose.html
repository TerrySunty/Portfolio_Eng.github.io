Who Are You

Game Design Studio Week 5 Project
（I was responsible for game development, mechanics design, part of narrative design.）

Demo Video
（Time：2min19s）



Theme

This prototype was finished in my master course Game Design Studio week 5, a game jam project in three days. The theme of Game Jam is Play to Lose,

Brainstorming：

Sometimes when players’ intentions go against the character’s intentions, they may hope to have a chance to choose to lose. Like many players' actions in the game This War of Mine. Based on this idea, we thought of Erlang Shen, a Chinese god who captured and imprisoned his sister because she married a mortal man (which was not allowed in the laws of gods). The majority of viewers of this story hope that Erlang Shen will never catch his sister. Therefore, we thought about designing a runner game based on this story, in which players could control Erlang Shen, and our players could have the chance to let the sister go.
But, since it is a Chinese myth and might cause some confusion to our players, we decided to change the background story for our game, and stick to the core point that players control the villain. The first version is about working for a tyrant.
Then we came up with a more familiar background - gangsters. And our main character changed from an undercover detective in a gang to a son of a gangster boss, in order to give our players more agency to choose what they want to do.

Brainstorming

Game Description： Who are You is a 3D, platform, runner and action game based on the core point “players control the villains and have agency to lose”. The story unfolds in contemporary days. A boy named X, who was a son of a gang boss but used to live a normal life without knowing what his father was doing, was forced to join the gang after the death of his father. Due to some reasons, he was just an enforcer team captain at the bottom of the gang chain and his main job was to catch people according to the orders of the current gang boss.

Gameplay：
In the game, the player needs to decide the protagonist’s behavior: catch the boss's enemies or let them go, to influence on 3 types of attribute values, to shape the protagonist’s characteristic. Different combinations of 3 types of values will lead to different game results. The player will always lose but will also always win at the same time. For example, if the player lets the boss’s enemies go, he may lose the boss's tolerance, and win the favor from innocent people. Thus, the player will always play to lose, but also play to win. Players need to make a trade-off.
There are two main parts of gameplay - Runner and Action.

Mechanics:
Player's Interaction：

  1. Movement：Run, Jump, Crouch
  2. Shoot with two types of bullets：One is to speed up the NPC，the other is to slow down the NPC.
  3. Close Combat：To catch NPC
  4. Making decision：Speed up the NPC and let the NPC go；Catch up with the NPC and fail in the fight to let the NPC run away；Catch the NPC

NPC行为：

  1. Movement
  2. Close Combat

Attribute Values：

  1. Tolerance of the Gang Boss (short as Tolerance hereinafter): 0-100, starts with 30.
  2. Information Gained by the Police (short as Information hereinafter): 0-100, starts with 0.
  3. Favorable Views among the Right (short as FV hereinafter): 0-100, starts with 50.

Settings of NPC：

  1. Common People: Who sees the drug trading process between your gang and another accidentally.
    • Captured: Tolerance +5, Information +0, FV -10.
    • Catch up but let the NPC go: Tolerance -5, Information +5, FV +10.
    • Let the NPC go without catching up: Tolerance -10, Information +5, FV +0.
  2. Police: Who is found doing undercover detective work in your gang.
    • Captured: Tolerance +15, Information -10, FV -10.
    • Catch up but let the NPC go: Tolerance -10, Information +10, FV +10.
    • Let the NPC go without catching up: Tolerance -15, Information +5, FV +0.
  3. Gangster in the other gang: Who runs away when his gang is fighting with yours.
    • Captured: Tolerance +10, Information +0, FV +0.
    • Catch up but let the NPC go (there is a 50% chance that this gangster will be caught by the police):
      • (Caught by the police)Tolerance -5, Information +10, FV +0;
      • (not caught by the police) Tolerance -5, Information +0, FV +0
    • Let the NPC go without catching up: Tolerance -10, Information +0, FV +0.

Win and Lose：

  1. Win-Lose，Win but lose: The player captures the innocent, gains the boss's trust, and achieves the goal. But the player loses the goodwill of others and is hated
  2. Lose-Win, lose but win: The player lets go of the NPC, loses the trust of the gang boss, and the boss is angry. But the NPC realizes the kindness of the player and has a good impression on the player.
